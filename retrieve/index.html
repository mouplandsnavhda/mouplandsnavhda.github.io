Retrieving

Learning how to create a system & problem solve

We want to start with the basics and drive towards the trained retrieve/force fetch/etc. Once we’re there my hope is you understand what’s happening and why it’s happening in the context of a finished retrieve.

The ultimate goal is for you to find simple answers to complex situations you find yourself in along the way.

Operant Conditioning

The ideal dog, one that’s trainable, understands that their actions have consequences both good and bad. We call that dog operant

Reinforcement(R) means the target behavior increases.

Punishment(P) means the target behavior decreases.

Positive(+) means I’m adding something

Negative(-) means I’m removing something

Examples:

  • Food
  • Ecollar
  • Ignore
  • Seat belt alarms
  • Hunger (and it’s relationship to food)

I can’t stress this enough - DO NOT PUNISH LACK OF BEHAVIOR. Punishment by definition exterminates behavior!

Don’t be a dictator and don’t be a vending machine. Be engaged. Be honest. Be fair.

Implementation

These things are extremely easy to measure. Just take notes.

If the trend isn’t in the desired direction, change what you’re doing. If the desired behavior isn’t increasing you're not reinforcing. If the undesired behavior isn’t decreasing, you aren’t punishing.

At the end of the day you’re going to end up spending more time understanding what they like and dislike. Once you really understand the dog in front of you can actually train things.  Don’t worry about training until you have the dog figured out.

Commands vs communication?

Dogs, like babies, do not understand english. You have to teach them if you want to communicate with them.


Communication examples

  • Ok/Free -> Do whatever you want, we’re done
  • Good -> Continue doing what you’re doing
  • Bad -> Stop what you’re doing
  • Yeaaaa/yes/super -> You did it! Nice work
  • Nooo -> Try again

Command examples

  • Place -> sit on the mat, or the floor is lava
  • Heel -> touch my left leg, or sit next to me(well how far is ok?)
  • Here/Come -> return back (in a straight line, can I pee, can I make a bend?)

Commands have conditions. Communication is feedback that makes the conditions clear.

Reinforcement examples using sit

  1. Luring a dogs nose into a sit w/ food
  2. Using leash pressure for a sit.
  3. Controlling the backend using a wall (manipulation of the environment to reduce variables)

Punishment examples using Snake Breaking/Counter Surfing

  1. Using the ecollar
  2. Stepping on a foot (place)
  3. Using your voice

Behavioral economics

The magnitude of the reinforcer is enough to support the expense of the behavior.

What you’ll find out is that what they like/dislike changes over time. Understanding economics is a shortcut to success.
